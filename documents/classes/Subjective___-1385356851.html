Class: Subjective

Annotations (1)

  • rdfs:comment "Uncertainty Derivation is considered subjective when it is assessed via a subjective judgment, e.g., an subject matter expert's estimation, a gambler's guess, etc. Note that even though one might use formal methods for this assessment, it is the assessment itself that defines the Uncertainty Derivation as subjective. For example, a meteorologist may follow a rather sophisticated procedure to establish a weather forecast, but if the numbers ultimately are derived from his judgment then it is a subjective uncertainty derivation. " @en
